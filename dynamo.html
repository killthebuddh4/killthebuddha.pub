dynamo

A dynamo is a device that generates electric power. I don't know anything more than that about what is a dynamo. I hear “dynamo” and in my head there's a whirling and an output. This format is also how the world works, in my head. Everybody knows the world is infinitely complex, but I'm not so sure. I have a feeling it's really just a whirling and an output. The world is a dynamo and I want to understand its whirling, its simple dynamics. I think it might be simple like a music might be simple even when it's complex. Music is emergent, it's a whirling and an output. If you dive into the core and apprehend the dynamics, you can intuit the whirling, you can do the things musicians do. Project dynamo is my attempt to develop and cultivate an intuitive understanding of the world's whirling. I know I will probably fail.

a starting point

First, consider the world as N semiautonomous interdependent regions. Then, for each region, consider the following questions:

What are the key geographical features? What kind of seasonal variation exists?

What are the most salient natural boons and banes to life? What makes life easy and what makes life hard?

How many people are there? How much food do they produce? How much water do they have access to? How much free energy is lying around?

What do they sell? Who do they sell it to? Who else sells the stuff? Between the buyers and sellers, who has the leverage?

What do they buy? Who do they buy it from? Between the buyers and sellers, who has the leverage?

Who are the economic power players, i.e who is doing the selling? Are they private or public? Are they foreign or domestic? If they're foreign, how much leverage do the locals have?

What does the infrastructure look like? How are the roads, the waterways, the power grid, and the education system? Are there any major universities? What kind of healthcare is available? What about financial services?

What are the major cultural denominations? Are they religious, ethnic, socioeconomic? What are the key differences between the region's populace and their neighbors'?

Who are the political power players? Whence do they derive their powers? What alliances and conflicts are they actively engaged in? What is the basic political architecture in the region? How much violence are they capable of? Do they have nukes?

Which of the answers 1-9 can be mostly explained given mostly geographic priors? Which answers only make sense when you remember some kind of historical path dependency?

How quickly are things changing? What are the catalysts? Are they endogenous or exogenous? Are they designed or mechanical?

Piece of cake. Now, where to start?

the global village construction set

I was tumbling down a solarpunk rabbit hole and discovered the Open Source Ecology (OSE) project and their global village construction set (GVCS). The GVCS is a collection of 50 machines that together could bootstrap a modern civilization. The OSE is on a mission to design, document, and develop fully open source versions of each of the 50 machines. So sick. Unfortunately it doesn't look like they're making a ton of progress (or maybe the scope is just so massive). But even if they don't succeed, I at least think the list itself is a great resource. If you want to scale the dynamo, what better anchor point than its 50 central machines!?
