solarpunk

I've never liked labels but solarpunk is starting to grow on me. I've always had a fuck you attitude towards institutional authority but a deep appreciation for institutions. This contradiction says a lot about me and the way I think, I think.

Recently I was reading Ivan Illich's Tools for Conviviality and it hit me: "This is what I want to do with my career, I want to build software tools for conviviality". Illich wrote in the 1970s, it may be the case that solarpunk tools markets better today, but the ideas seem to spring from the same source and flow in the same direction.

Illich uses the term tool to mean (basically) any synthetic thing. This includes hammers and computers but also institutions, conventions, and culture. I want to build tools that amplify human talent but don't obviate it. To some extent, this is impossible. To some extent, it's necessary.

I want to understand what amplifies and what obviates, and how much, and what are the second- and higher-order effects, and which modern institutions are "solarpunk compatible" and which are not, and what technology is being developed, and what scientific breakthroughs are needed, and how can I orientate myself and my family towards a more convivial or solarpunk future, and... well, I'll stop there. I have a lot of questions and a lot of reading to do, maybe a lifetime's worth.
